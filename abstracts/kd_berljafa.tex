For given matrices $\lbrace A, F\rbrace\subset\mathbb{C}^{N\times N}$
and a vector $\mathbf v\in\mathbb{C}^N$,
we consider the problem
of finding a rational function $R_m^{\min}$ of type $(m, m)$ such that
\begin{equation*}
  %R_m^{\star} = \mathop{\operatorname{argmin}}_{R_m}\|F\mathbf v-R_m^{}(A)\mathbf v\|_2^2,
  \|F\mathbf v-R_m^{}(A)\mathbf v\|_2^2 \to \min,
\end{equation*}
and propose an iterative algorithm \cite{BeGu14, BeGu15}
for its solution.
At each iteration the algorithm constructs a rational Krylov space
$\mathcal{Q}_{m+1}(A, \mathbf v)$ and manipulates
an associated Arnoldi decomposition
to find better approximations to the
poles of $R_m^{\min}$.
In the special case when
$A=\operatorname{diag}(\lambda_j^{})$ and $F=\operatorname{diag}(\psi_j^{})$
are diagonal we have a weighted rational least squares fitting problem
\begin{math}
  \sum_{j=1}^{N}|v_j|^2\cdot|\psi_j^{}-R_m^{}(\lambda_j^{})|^2 \to \min,
\end{math}
and compare our method to the popular \emph{vector fitting} \cite{GuSe99}.
